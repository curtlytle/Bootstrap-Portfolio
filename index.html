About Me

I was raised in Pueblo Colorado. I graduated from BYU in 1992 with a BS in Mechanical Engineering with an emphasis in technology. I married my sweetheart in 1988 and have two children


My first job out of college was working for Bath Iron Works (BIW), in Maine. BIW built Navy destroyers and I was employed in their IT department writing supporting software for the ship designers. That is how I started my career in the software industry, and have been developing software ever since. Currently my goal is to become a true full stack web developer, where most of my career has been in the Java stack, backend frameworks with JDBC, Hibernate, Servlets and Struts frameworks. My career took a detour by going into the management world. With that my time was more focused on improving my management skills, along with project management, which I tried to incorporate the agile methodologies of Scrum as my style of managment. After 5 years in the management arena, I find myself back into the technical world and enjoying the software career again.


I am currently taking the University of Utah full stack bootcamp course to help me quickly get up to speed on the new Javascript frameworks and Javascript itself. In the course, I hope to become a proficient Javascript web developer, learning frameworks like MongoDB, ExpressJS, ReactJS, and NodeJS, along with becoming proficient the latest of HTML5 and CSS3.


Along with that, my current position is doing all Java, with Swing and Hibernate. I am also getting up to speed with the new Java 8 features and refreshing my skills with the Spring frameworks.
